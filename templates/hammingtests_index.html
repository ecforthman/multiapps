Hamming Tests

The dynamic piece of this demonstration consists of the input of two strings to a form that are submitted for evaluation, the result is returned and saved to a database. This result is a hamming distance. The last five results may be viewed, simultaneously.

A hamming distance is a position by position comparision of two equal length strings with the resulting sum being the number of non-matching values. The strings can be any sequence of characters or numbers. The concept of a hamming distance was introduced in 1950 by Richard Hamming in his paper Error detecting and error correcting codes.
